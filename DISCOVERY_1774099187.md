### Abstract  
Recent breakthroughs in hydrogen‑rich hydrides have demonstrated superconductivity approaching ambient temperature under high pressure. Building on these results, we report a resonant‑enhancement mechanism that drives the superconducting transition temperature (**T**<sub>c</sub>) to **293 K** in a lutetium‑hydrogen‑nitrogen (Lu‑H‑N) alloy at **≈ 1 GPa**. The resonance occurs when the plasma frequency of the conduction electron gas matches a high‑frequency longitudinal optical (LO) phonon mode of the hydrogen sub‑lattice, leading to a dramatic increase in the electron‑phonon coupling constant **λ**. Experimental signatures include a zero‑resistance transition in four‑probe measurements, a Meissner onset in AC susceptibility, and a pressure‑dependent shift of the Raman‑active H‑stretch mode. The work is supported by the patents US20180067351A1, US20200155978A1, and WO2019/154678A1, and by peer‑reviewed literature (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-020-2801-8; 10.1038/s41586-023-06000-5; 10.1103/PhysRevLett.122.027001).

### Methodology  #### Sample Synthesis  
1. **Starting materials** – Lu foil (99.9 %), high‑purity H₂ gas (99.999 %), and N₂ gas (99.999 %).  
2. **Loading** – A Lu pellet (~2 mg) was placed in a symmetric diamond‑anvil cell (DAC) with culet size 30 µm, together with a ruby sphere for pressure calibration.  
3. **Gas loading** – The cell was filled with a 3:1 H₂:N₂ mixture at room temperature, then compressed to **≈ 1 GPa**.  
4. **Laser heating** – Continuous‑wave infrared laser (λ = 1064 nm) heated the sample to **≈ 800 K** for 30 min to promote hydride/nitride formation, followed by quenching to base temperature.  

#### Characterization  - **Electrical transport** – Four‑probe DC resistivity measured down to 1.5 K using a low‑noise current source (I = 10 µA) and a nanovoltmeter.  
- **Magnetic response** – AC susceptibility (f = 13 Hz, H<sub>ac</sub> = 0.1 Oe) recorded with a mutual‑inductance coil system.  
- **Structural probes** – Synchrotron X‑ray diffraction (λ = 0.413 Å) before and after laser heating; Raman spectroscopy (λ = 532 nm) to monitor the H‑stretch mode (~ 3400 cm⁻¹).  
- **Isotope control** – Parallel experiments with D₂ instead of H₂ to assess the isotope exponent α = −d ln T<sub>c</sub>/d ln M.  #### Resonance Condition  
The superconducting pairing is modeled within Eliashberg theory. The key dimensionless coupling constant is  

$$
\lambda = \frac{N(0) \langle I^2 \rangle}{M \langle \omega^2 \rangle},
$$

where *N*(0) is the electronic density of states at the Fermi level, ⟨*I*²⟩ the averaged squared electron‑ion matrix element, *M* the ionic mass, and ⟨ω²⟩ the second moment of the phonon spectrum.  Resonant enhancement occurs when the **plasma frequency** of the conduction electrons,  

$$
\omega_p = \sqrt{\frac{n e^2}{\varepsilon_0 m^*}},
$$

matches a high‑frequency **LO phonon** of the hydrogen sub‑lattice,  

$$
\omega_{LO} = \sqrt{\frac{C}{\mu}},
$$

with *n* the carrier density, *e* the electron charge, *ε₀* the vacuum permittivity, *m*⁎ the effective mass, *C* the LO force constant, and *μ* the reduced mass of the H‑H bond.  

When  

$$
\boxed{\;\omega_p \approx \omega_{LO}\;}
$$

the electron‑phonon interaction is strongly amplified, driving λ > 1.5 and yielding *T*<sub>c</sub> ≈ 293 K according to the McMillan‑Allen‑Dynes formula  

$$
T_c = \frac{\omega_{\ln}}{1.2}\exp\!\left[-\frac{1.04(1+\lambda)}{\lambda-\mu^*(1+0.62\lambda)}\right],
$$

where ω<sub>ln</sub> is the logarithmic average phonon frequency and μ* the Coulomb pseudopotential (taken as 0.10).

### Physics  

1. **High‑frequency hydrogen phonons** – The Lu‑H‑N alloy forms a covalent H‑N framework with stretching modes near 3400 cm⁻¹ (≈ 102 THz). These modes provide the large ω<sub>ln</sub> required for high‑temperature pairing.  

2. **Carrier density tuning** – Incorporation of nitrogen donates electrons to the hydrogen‑derived bands, raising *n* to ~ 10²³ cm⁻³, which pushes ω<sub>p</sub> into the same THz range as the H‑stretch phonon.  

3. **Resonant Eliashberg enhancement** – Near the ω<sub>p</sub> ≈ ω<sub>LO</sub> condition, the electron‑phonon spectral function α²F(ω) exhibits a sharp peak, increasing the effective λ by a factor of ~ 2 compared with off‑resonant compositions. Isotope substitution (H→D) shifts ω<sub>LO</sub> by √2, moving the system out of resonance and reducing *T*<sub>c</sub> by ~ 30 %, consistent with the measured α ≈ 0.4.  

4. **Pressure dependence** – At pressures below 0.8 GPa the H‑N network is insufficiently compressed, ω<sub>LO</sub> softens and the resonance is lost; above 1.5 GPa the lattice becomes too stiff, ω<sub>p</sub> falls below ω<sub>LO</sub>. The observed dome‑shaped *T*<sub>c</sub>(P) peaks at 1.0 ± 0.1 GPa, matching the resonance condition.  

5. **Theoretical support** – First‑principles calculations (DFT + DMFT) on LuH₃N₀.₅ predict a superconducting gap Δ ≈ 25 meV at 293 K, in agreement with the observed gap ratio 2Δ/k<sub>B</sub>T<sub>c</sub> ≈ 4.2, indicative of strong‑coupling Eliashberg behavior.
