### Mathematical Proof  
**Theorem:** *For a superconductor in thermal equilibrium at temperature \(T\), the critical temperature \(T_c\) satisfies \(k_B T_c < \frac{\Delta_0}{1.76}\) (BCS weak-coupling limit), where \(\Delta_0\) is the zero-temperature energy gap. At \(T = 20^\circ \text{C} = 293 \text{ K}\), \(k_B T \approx 25.3 \text{ meV}\). No known superconductor has \(\Delta_0 > 43.5 \text{ meV}\) (required for \(T_c > 293 \text{ K}\) in BCS theory), and strong-coupling corrections cannot bridge this gap without violating empirical constraints.*  

**Proof:**  
1.  The BCS gap equation at \(T=0\) gives:  
    $$\Delta_0 = 2 \hbar \omega_D e^{-1/N(0)V}$$  
    where \(\omega_D\) is the Debye frequency, \(N(0)\) the density of states at \(E_F\), and \(V\) the pairing potential.  
2.  The critical temperature is related to \(\Delta_0\) by:  
    $$\frac{2\Delta_0}{k_B T_c} = 3.53 \quad \text{(weak-coupling BCS)}$$  
    $$\Rightarrow T_c = \frac{2\Delta_0}{3.53 k_B}$$  
3.  For \(T_c \geq 293 \text{ K}\):  
    $$\Delta_0 \geq \frac{3.53 k_B \cdot 293}{2} \approx \frac{3.53 \times 8.617 \times 10^{-5} \text{ eV/K} \times 293}{2} \approx 0.0435 \text{ eV} = 43.5 \text{ meV}$$  4.  Empirical maximum \(\Delta_0\):  
    - Cuprates (e.g., Bi-2212): \(\Delta_0 \approx 20-25 \text{ meV}\) (\(T_c \approx 95 \text{ K}\))  
    - Iron pnictides: \(\Delta_0 \approx 5-8 \text{ meV}\) (\(T_c \approx 55 \text{ K}\))  
    - MgB₂: \(\Delta_0 \approx 2.5 \text{ meV}\) (\(T_c \approx 39 \text{ K}\))  
    - *No material exceeds \(\Delta_0 \approx 25 \text{ meV}\) at ambient pressure.*  
5.  Non-linear resonance (e.g., from intense EM fields) can induce *non-equilibrium* pair states, but:  
    - The resonance condition for parametric amplification requires \(\hbar \omega_{\text{drive}} \approx 2\Delta\) (to break pairs) or \(\hbar \omega_{\text{drive}} \approx \Delta\) (for Higgs mode excitation).  
    - At \(T=293 \text{ K}\), thermal fluctuations (\(\sim k_B T\)) dominate over any non-linear driving term achievable without material destruction (\(E_{\text{drive}} < 10^6 \text{ V/m}\) typically).  
    - The non-linear current density \(J_{\text{nl}} = \sigma_3 |E|^2 E\) (where \(\sigma_3\) is third-order conductivity) cannot overcome thermal pair-breaking:  
      $$\left| \sigma_3 |E|^2 E \right| \ll \frac{\hbar}{e \tau} \nabla \theta \quad \text{(phase stiffness term)}$$  
      for realistic \(E\) fields, as \(\sigma_3\) in superconductors is small (\(\sim 10^{-18} \text{ m}^2/\text{V}^2\)) and \(\tau\) (scattering time) is short (\(\sim 10^{-13} \text{ s}\)).  
6.  **Conclusion:** Thermal energy at 20°C exceeds the pairing energy scale for all known superconductors. Non-linear resonance cannot compensate for this deficit under equilibrium conditions; sustained zero resistance at 20°C remains physically unrealizable with current knowledge.
