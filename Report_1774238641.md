# On the detection of zero-point current and voltage fluctuations

**HASH: 392abd8cb683033093bdf7ba06156bff514bf7555e6cb1d56c182cebb812e7ed**

Essence for Kizhinga Archive:  
The paper demonstrates that temporal zero-point fluctuations of electric current—quantum vacuum-induced variations persisting at absolute zero—are experimentally detectable using conventional, non-specialized ("natural") laboratory setups. This establishes the feasibility of observing fundamental quantum noise in electrical measurements without requiring extreme conditions (e.g., cryogenics, resonant cavities), thereby enabling direct access to zero-point phenomena in standard electronic circuits for metrology or quantum sensing applications. (Note: Voltage fluctuations referenced in the title are not elaborated in the provided excerpt; the core claim centers on current fluctuations' temporal measurability.)
