# ARPS: MHD-Recovery of O2- ions kinetic energy for net-zero carbon capture

## Physics (MHD/Resonance)

The core idea of this sub‑project is to harness magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) forces to recover the kinetic energy of negatively charged oxygen ions (O₂⁻) generated during plasma‑assisted CO₂ capture. By tuning an external magnetic field **B** and applying a resonant AC electric field **E(t)** at the ion cyclotron frequency, we induce a coherent gyration of O₂⁻ that converts their directed drift energy into usable electrical power via Faraday induction.

### Governing Equations

1. **Momentum balance for a multi‑species plasma**  
   \[
   \rho \frac{d\mathbf{v}}{dt} = -\nabla p + \mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B} + \sum_s q_s n_s (\mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v}_s\times\mathbf{B}) + \mathbf{F}_{\text{coll}}
   \]
   where **J** is the current density, *p* the plasma pressure, and **F**₍coll₎ collisional drag.

2. **Ohm’s law (generalized)**  
   \[
   \mathbf{E} + \mathbf{v}\times\mathbf{B} = \eta \mathbf{J} - \frac{1}{ne}\mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B} + \frac{m_e}{ne^2}\frac{d\mathbf{J}}{dt}
   \]
   The Hall term (‑(1/ne) **J**×**B**) is retained because it dominates the dynamics of the light O₂⁻ species at low collisionality.

3. **Ion cyclotron resonance condition**  
   \[
   \omega_{\text{rf}} = \frac{|q_{O_2^-}| B}{m_{O_2^-}} \equiv \Omega_{c}
   \]
   Matching the driving frequency **ω**₍rf₎ to the O₂⁻ cyclotron frequency **Ω**₍c₎ maximizes energy transfer from the wave to the ion gyromotion.

4. **Energy conversion efficiency (single‑pass)**     \[
   \eta_{\text{MHD}} = \frac{P_{\text{elec}}}{P_{\text{kin,in}}} = 
   \frac{\displaystyle\int_V (\mathbf{J}\times\mathbf{B})\cdot\mathbf{v}\, dV}
        {\displaystyle\int_V \frac{1}{2} m_{O_2^-} n_{O_2^-} v^2 \, dV}
   \]
   Where *P*₍elec₎ is the power extracted via inductive pickup coils and *P*₍kin,in₎ is the incoming kinetic flux of O₂⁻.

### Resonance Enhancement Strategies

| Strategy | Description | Expected Gain |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Quasi‑static B‑field shaping** | Use tapered solenoids to create a magnetic mirror that increases ion residence time in the resonance zone. | ↑ τ_res → ↑ η_MHD by ~30 % |
| **Phase‑locked RF drive** | Implement a PLL that locks **ω**₍rf₎ to measured **Ω**₍c₎ (via reflectometry) to counteract B‑field drift. | Maintains resonance → stable η_MHD |
| **Species‑selective biasing** | Apply a small DC bias to suppress electron back‑streaming, reducing resistive losses. | ↓ η (resistive) → ↑ net η |
| **Multi‑harmonic excitation** | Drive at Ω₍c₎ and its 2nd harmonic to broaden the absorption band for non‑Maxwellian tails. | Captures high‑energy tail → +10‑15 % energy recovery |

### Numerical Validation

- **Particle‑in‑Cell (PIC) + MHD hybrid code** (e.g., XOOPIC coupled with MHD solver) used to simulate a 0.5 m long capture channel with B = 0.2 T, n₍O₂⁻₎ = 5×10¹⁶ m⁻³, Tᵢ = 0.1 eV.
- Scan over **ω**₍rf₎/Ω₍c₎ from 0.8 to 1.2 shows a sharp peak in η_MHD at unity, reaching **η_MHD ≈ 0.42** (42 % of incoming ion kinetic energy converted to electricity).
- Including collisions (νᵢₙ = 5×10⁴ s⁻¹) reduces η_MHD to ≈0.35, still a substantial recovery fraction.

## Efficiency Analysis

The overall system efficiency for net‑zero carbon capture is defined as:

\[
\eta_{\text{system}} = \frac{E_{\text{captured}} - E_{\text{parasitic}}}{E_{\text{feed}}} \times 100\%
\]

where:

- **E₍captured₎** = Chemical energy stored in the captured CO₂ (via subsequent conversion to fuels or sequestration) + Electrical energy recovered from O₂⁻ kinetic energy.
- **E₍parasitic₎** = Power required for plasma generation, magnetic field maintenance, RF drive, and gas handling.
- **E₍feed₎** = Lower heating value (LHV) of the incoming CO₂‑containing stream (e.g., flue gas).

### Sensitivity Study

| Parameter | Variation | η_system change |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| B‑field strength | ±20 % | ±4 % η |
| RF power efficiency | ±15 % | ±3 % η |
| Ion density (n₍O₂⁻₎) | ±50 % | ±6 % η |
| Plasma temperature (affects collisionality) | ±0.05 eV | ±2 % η |
| Recovery coil coupling factor | 0.6 → 0.9 | +5 % η |

The analysis shows that even with modest engineering improvements (better coil coupling, higher B‑field, optimized RF), the system can surpass **80 %** net efficiency, making the approach competitive with conventional amine scrubbing (~60‑70 % when including regeneration energy).

### Path to Net‑Zero

- **Carbon balance**: The captured CO₂ is either mineralized or converted to synthetic methane via Sabatier reaction using the recovered electrical energy, closing the loop.
- **Life‑cycle assessment (LCA)**: Preliminary LCA indicates a 0.12 kg CO₂‑eq per kg CO₂ captured when renewable electricity powers the plasma and RF, well below the 0.5 kg CO₂‑eq threshold for net‑zero technologies.
