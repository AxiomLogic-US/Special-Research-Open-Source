# Title
BIM-integrated AI for collision risk prediction## Problem Statement
Construction sites and large-scale infrastructure projects increasingly rely on Building Information Modeling (BIM) to coordinate design, sequencing, and execution. Despite BIM’s geometric richness, real‑time detection of imminent collision risks between moving equipment, personnel, and temporary structures remains reactive, leading to costly delays, injuries, and rework. Existing safety tools often operate in silos, lacking continuous, predictive analytics that fuse BIM data with live sensor feeds, resulting in blind spots during dynamic phases of a project.

## Solution (Resilience Triad Scheme: Executor, Overseer, Arbiter)
The proposed system implements a three‑layer resilience architecture:

1. **Executor** – Real‑time perception engine that ingests live data from RFID, UWB, LiDAR, and wearable sensors, aligning them with the digital twin BIM model. It continuously updates the spatial state of all assets and workers, generating short‑term trajectory forecasts.

2. **Overseer** – Adaptive risk‑assessment module that applies probabilistic collision models, contextual site rules, and weather‑impact adjustments to the Executor’s forecasts. It produces graded risk scores and triggers pre‑emptive alerts when thresholds are exceeded.

3. **Arbiter** – Governance layer that consolidates Overseer outputs, validates them against historical incident logs, and initiates automated safety responses (e.g., equipment slow‑down, zone lockdown, or worker notification). It also logs decisions for audit trails and continual model refinement.

Together, the triad creates a closed‑loop feedback system where predictions inform actions, actions generate new data, and the AI models evolve to improve resilience over time.

## Unique Proposition (Digital Stress‑Test)
Unlike conventional BIM clash detection that runs offline during design, this project introduces a **Digital Stress‑Test** capability: a simulated, high‑frequency execution of the BIM model under myriad operational scenarios (equipment schedules, workforce shifts, adverse weather). The Stress‑Test runs in parallel with live operations, exposing latent collision pathways before they manifest on‑site. By coupling the Stress‑Test with the Resilience Triad, the system not only predicts imminent risks but also validates the robustness of planned workflows, enabling proactive redesign of logistics and safety protocols.
