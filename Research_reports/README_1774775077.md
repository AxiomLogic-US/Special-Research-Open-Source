# Compliance-AI for Regulatory Risk Management in Nuclear Projects

## Problem Statement
Nuclear facilities operate under one of the most stringent regulatory regimes in the world. Compliance officers must continuously interpret evolving statutes, safety standards, and licensing conditions while managing massive volumes of operational data, inspection reports, and incident logs. Manual review processes are slow, error‑prone, and unable to keep pace with the rapid introduction of new technologies (e.g., SMRs, advanced fuels). This creates three critical pain points:

1. **Regulatory Blind Spots** – Delayed detection of non‑conformities can lead to costly enforcement actions or, worse, safety incidents.  
2. **Resource Overload** – Compliance teams spend excessive time on repetitive data extraction and correlation tasks, diverting expertise from strategic risk mitigation.  
3. **Inconsistent Decision‑Making** – Variability in how different auditors interpret the same rule undermines confidence in compliance outcomes and complicates licensing renewals.

## Solution (Resilience Triad Scheme: Executor, Overseer, Arbiter)
Compliance‑AI implements a three‑layered resilience architecture that mirrors the classic safety‑triad concept but is tailored for regulatory risk management.

| Layer | Role | Core Functions |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **Executor** | Autonomous data‑agent | • Ingests heterogeneous streams (sensor telemetry, maintenance logs, regulatory feeds, international IAEA updates).<br>• Performs real‑time normalization, tagging, and semantic enrichment using domain‑specific ontologies.<br>• Generates preliminary compliance indicators (e.g., “dose‑rate exceedance probability”, “license condition drift score”). |
| **Overseer** | Adaptive reasoning engine | • Applies probabilistic rule‑based inference and machine‑learning classifiers to the Executor’s indicators.<br>• Detects anomalous patterns, predicts emerging regulatory gaps, and assigns confidence scores.<br>• Triggers alerts to human analysts when uncertainty exceeds calibrated thresholds. |
| **Arbiter** | Governance & decision layer | • Presents concise, evidence‑backed recommendations to compliance officers and licensing authorities.<br>• Maintains an immutable audit trail of all AI‑derived conclusions, supporting traceability for audits.<br>• Enables closed‑loop learning: outcomes of regulatory inspections feed back to refine Executor models and Overseer rule weights. |

The triad ensures **defense‑in‑depth**: raw data is continuously vetted (Executor), interpreted with uncertainty awareness (Overseer), and finally validated through transparent governance (Arbiter). Human experts remain in the loop for final judgments, while the AI handles the heavy lifting of scale and speed.

## Unique Proposition (Digital Stress‑Test)
Unlike conventional compliance tracking tools, Compliance‑AI incorporates a **Digital Stress‑Test** module that simulates regulatory scenarios under varying operational and external conditions:

- **What‑If Engine**: Runs thousands of simulated futures (e.g., sudden regulatory amendment, seismic event, supply‑chain disruption) to quantify the probability of non‑compliance.
- **Risk Heat‑Mapping**: Visualizes stress‑test results across plant systems, time horizons, and regulatory domains, highlighting latent vulnerabilities before they manifest.
- **Adaptive Calibration**: Stress‑test outcomes automatically tune the Executor’s anomaly detectors and the Overseer’s decision thresholds, ensuring the system stays resilient to evolving risk landscapes.

This proactive, simulation‑driven approach transforms compliance from a reactive checkpoint into a continuous, anticipatory safety culture component.
