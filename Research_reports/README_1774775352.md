# Title
Compliance‑AI for regulatory risk management in nuclear projects

## Problem Statement
Nuclear projects face a complex web of national and international regulations, safety standards, and licensing requirements that evolve throughout the project lifecycle. Manual compliance tracking is labor‑intensive, error‑prone, and often reacts only after deviations are discovered, leading to costly delays, rework, and potential safety gaps. Stakeholders struggle to maintain a real‑time view of regulatory exposure, to predict the impact of design changes, and to demonstrate continual adherence to oversight bodies.

## Solution (Resilience Triad Scheme: Executor, Overseer, Arbiter)
Compliance‑AI implements a three‑layer resilience architecture:

- **Executor** – An AI‑driven automation layer that continuously ingests design documents, operational data, and regulatory feeds. It maps requirements to system components, generates compliance evidence, and triggers corrective actions when thresholds are approached.
- **Overseer** – A monitoring subsystem that watches the Executor’s outputs for anomalies, drift, or conflicting interpretations. It uses explainable AI techniques to surface uncertainties and to flag areas where human judgment is required.
- **Arbiter** – A governance layer composed of domain experts and regulatory advisors who review Overseer alerts, validate AI recommendations, and enforce policy updates. The Arbiter also feeds learned decisions back into the Executor to improve future performance.

Together, the Triad creates a closed‑loop system that prevents, detects, and resolves regulatory risks before they escalate.

## Unique Proposition (Digital Stress‑Test)
Compliance‑AI incorporates a proprietary **Digital Stress‑Test** engine that simulates regulatory scenarios—such as new safety directives, licensing amendments, or emergency‑response mandates—against a virtual twin of the nuclear project. By running thousands of what‑if analyses, the platform quantifies vulnerability scores, recommends pre‑emptive design adjustments, and produces audit‑ready documentation that demonstrates proactive compliance under extreme conditions.

## Economic Benefits
- **Reduced compliance overhead:** Automation cuts manual tracking effort by up to 60 %, freeing engineering resources for core design work.
- **Avoided rework and delays:** Early detection of regulatory conflicts cuts potential schedule slips by an average of 3–6 months per major milestone.
- **Lower licensing risk:** Continuous evidence generation improves audit success rates, decreasing the likelihood of costly licensing penalties or enforcement actions.
- **Enhanced decision confidence:** Quantitative stress‑test results enable data‑driven trade‑offs between design options, optimizing capital expenditure while maintaining safety margins.
- **Scalable governance:** The Triad architecture supports reuse across multiple sites and fleets, amortizing development costs over a portfolio of nuclear projects.
