# BIM-integrated AI for Collision Risk Prediction

## Problem Statement
Construction sites are complex, dynamic environments where heavy equipment, personnel, and temporary structures constantly move and interact. Traditional safety monitoring relies on manual inspections, rule‑based alerts, and limited sensor data, which often fail to anticipate low‑probability, high‑impact collisions until after they occur. This reactive approach leads to costly downtime, injuries, legal liabilities, and erosion of stakeholder trust. A proactive, data‑driven method that continuously evaluates spatial and temporal risk factors is needed to prevent accidents before they happen.

## Solution (Resilience Triad Scheme: Executor, Overseer, Arbiter)
The proposed system embeds artificial intelligence directly into Building Information Modeling (BIM) workflows, forming a three‑layer resilience architecture:

1. **Executor** – Real‑time perception module that fuses BIM geometry with live sensor streams (LiDAR, RFID, UWB, and computer vision) to generate an up‑to‑date digital twin of the site. It continuously updates object trajectories, occlusion maps, and environmental conditions.

2. **Overseer** – Predictive analytics engine that runs physics‑based simulations and machine‑learning models on the Executor’s output to forecast collision probabilities for all moving agents over configurable horizons (seconds to minutes). It produces risk heatmaps, early‑warning alerts, and suggested mitigations (e.g., speed adjustments, path reroutes).

3. **Arbiter** – Governance layer that evaluates the Overseer’s recommendations against project safety policies, regulatory constraints, and operational priorities. It decides whether to issue automatic interventions, notify human supervisors, or log the event for further analysis, ensuring that actions remain aligned with overall project goals and safety culture.

The triad operates in a closed loop: the Executor feeds fresh data to the Overseer, the Overseer refines risk estimates, the Arbiter validates and enacts responses, and the outcomes are fed back to improve the Executor’s perception models.

## Unique Proposition (Digital Stress‑Test)
Unlike conventional BIM‑based clash detection, which focuses on static design conflicts, this project introduces a **Digital Stress‑Test** capability. By simulating thousands of “what‑if” scenarios—varying equipment speeds, worker behaviors, weather conditions, and temporary layout changes—the system quantifies the site’s resilience to collision risks under extreme but plausible conditions. The stress‑test outputs a **Resilience Score** that project managers can track over time, enabling data‑driven decisions about resource allocation, schedule buffers, and safety investments before ground is broken.

## Economic Benefits
- **Accident Reduction:** Early prediction and prevention of collisions can lower incident rates by up to 40 %, directly reducing medical costs, workers’ compensation claims, and project delays.
- **Downtime Minimization:** Avoiding work stoppages preserves critical path integrity, saving an estimated $150 K–$300 K per day of avoided schedule slip on large‑scale projects.
- **Insurance Premium Optimization:** Demonstrated proactive risk management can lead to lower builder’s risk insurance premiums, yielding savings of 5‑10 % annually.
- **Resource Efficiency:** Optimized equipment routing and task sequencing derived from predictive insights decrease fuel consumption and idle time, cutting operational expenses by roughly 5‑8 %.
- **Enhanced Reputation:** A stronger safety record improves client confidence and can increase win rates for future contracts, contributing to long‑term revenue growth.
